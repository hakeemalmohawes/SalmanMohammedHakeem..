ü§ù 1. Complementary Operation

GFM inverters create the voltage and frequency reference for a power system. GFL inverters rely on that reference to inject synchronized current. In a hybrid system, GFMs form the grid, and GFLs follow it. This allows the GFLs to operate effectively even in the absence of traditional synchronous machines.

  • Utilizes the cost-efficiency of GFLs while maintaining grid stability with GFMs

To ensure stable operation, GFMs and GFLs must be properly coordinated:

  • Control loop interactions can lead to oscillations
  • Inertia mismatch may affect fault response
  • Protection systems must recognize both inverter types

  • GFMs provide grid support; GFLs deliver power
