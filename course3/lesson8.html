1. The Need for Coordination

Modern grids may include dozens or hundreds of inverters operating together. Coordinating GFM and GFL inverters becomes essential for frequency regulation, voltage support, and power sharing ‚Äî especially during transients and load changes.

2. Duality as a Design Tool

Duality allows us to structure systems so that GFM inverters serve as "anchors" and GFL inverters as "followers." This separation simplifies the design of coordination layers, stabilizes frequency response, and minimizes communication needs.

3. Design Considerations

  • Anchor Density: Adequate GFM presence is required to keep system synchronized
  • Follower Responsiveness: GFL units must rapidly and stably follow voltage references
  • Hierarchical Control: Decentralized GFM control + centralized GFL coordination improves scalability

  • Coordination is critical in systems with many inverters
  • Duality helps structure GFM-GFL interaction into anchor-follower dynamics
  • Designs should balance GFM strength with GFL responsiveness
