1. GFM Synchronization Approaches

Grid-Forming inverters do not rely on the external grid for synchronization. Instead, they generate their own voltage and frequency reference, often through droop control or virtual oscillator control (VOC). These methods allow them to autonomously synchronize and provide system stability.

2. GFL Synchronization Using PLLs

Grid-Following inverters synchronize using a Phase-Locked Loop (PLL). The PLL estimates the phase angle of the grid voltage and adjusts the inverter output accordingly. However, PLLs can become unstable under weak-grid or low-inertia conditions.

3. Comparison of Techniques

  • Droop Control: Mimics governor response; smooth and scalable
  • Virtual Oscillator Control: Nonlinear method with fast dynamic response
  • PLL: Linear, widely used, but sensitive to grid disturbances
