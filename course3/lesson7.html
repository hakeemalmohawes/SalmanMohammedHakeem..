1. Grid Code Evolution

Traditional grid codes were built around synchronous machines. As inverter-based resources grow, codes are evolving to address fast-acting, programmable sources. Grid-forming and grid-following inverters must now meet new standards for fault ride-through, voltage support, and frequency regulation.

2. Duality and Compliance

Duality clarifies how GFM and GFL inverters must be modeled and tested. GFM units must prove autonomous voltage/frequency control under disturbance, while GFL units must show stability under weak-grid and high-harmonic scenarios.

3. Emerging Standards

  • IEEE 2800: Specifies performance for inverter-based resources on transmission networks
  • Grid-forming requirements: Defined control response under fault, reconnection, and islanding
  • Validation: Includes HIL (Hardware-in-the-loop) and model-based testing
