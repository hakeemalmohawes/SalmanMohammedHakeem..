1. Harmonic Generation in Converters

Switching actions in converters create harmonics due to non-sinusoidal waveforms. These harmonics can degrade power quality, stress components, and interfere with control performance if not properly mitigated.

2. Common Filter Types

  • L filters: Simple and compact, but limited high-frequency attenuation
  • LC filters: Provide stronger attenuation at a specific corner frequency
  • LCL filters: Widely used in grid-connected converters, offering better damping of switching harmonics

3. Filter Modeling Techniques

Filters are typically modeled in the frequency domain using transfer functions or impedance representations. Key factors include:

  • Resonant frequencies and damping characteristics
  • Interaction with converter control loops
  • Effect on stability and phase margin
