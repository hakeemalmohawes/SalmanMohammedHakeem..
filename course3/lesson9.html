1. Importance of Simulation

Before deploying inverter-based systems, simulation is used to evaluate stability, performance, and control interactions. Testing GFM and GFL roles under dynamic scenarios helps refine control design and dispatch strategies.

2. Duality and Testing Strategy

By leveraging duality, system behavior under disturbances can be explored in a structured way. GFM and GFL inverters are modeled with mirrored assumptions, allowing scalable test design for system-level behavior under fault, load steps, and grid disconnection.

3. Common Tools and Methods

  • Time-domain simulation: Evaluate frequency and voltage dynamics
  • Hardware-in-the-loop (HIL): Integrates real control hardware into virtual grids
  • RTDS and EMT tools: Offer fast, real-time dynamic response analysis
