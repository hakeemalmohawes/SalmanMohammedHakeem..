1. Overview of Converter Topologies

High-power converters are built using a range of topologies depending on the application, power level, and desired control features. Key topologies include:

  • Voltage Source Converter (VSC): Widely used in HVdc, FACTS, and wind power due to its controllability
  • Current Source Converter (CSC): Used in traditional HVdc and some industrial drives
  • Modular Multilevel Converter (MMC): Provides excellent scalability and harmonic performance

2. Functional Principles

Each topology performs voltage and current conversion using semiconductor switches (IGBTs, GTOs, etc.). The switching strategy and filter design affect power quality, control dynamics, and overall system behavior.

3. Impact on Modeling

  • VSCs are modeled as voltage-controlled systems with PWM dynamics
  • CSCs are modeled as current-regulated systems with commutation constraints
  • MMCs require submodule-level modeling and averaging for system studies
