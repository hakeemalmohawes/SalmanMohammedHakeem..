1. Recap of Analytical Modeling Techniques

Throughout this course, we covered state-space modeling, impedance methods, linearization, filter design, and stability analysis. These tools are foundational for understanding and controlling modern power converter systems.

2. Emerging Trends

  • Grid-forming controls: Replacing synchronous machines with software-based grid support
  • Adaptive and AI-based control: Enhancing robustness and adaptability
  • Digital twins and HIL testing: Merging real-time simulation with physical control systems

3. Future Challenges

As grids become increasingly inverter-dominated, key challenges include:

  • Ensuring interoperability between devices and vendors
  • Maintaining stability under low inertia and fast dynamics
  • Scaling control strategies for distributed energy systems
