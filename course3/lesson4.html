1. Coordination Between Inverter Types

In a system with both GFM and GFL inverters, coordination is crucial. GFM inverters provide the grid reference, while GFL inverters inject current in response. Effective coordination ensures that all inverters contribute to power sharing without conflict or instability.

2. Power Sharing Mechanisms

Power sharing in GFM inverters is typically handled using droop control, which adjusts frequency and voltage as a function of active and reactive power output. GFL inverters regulate current magnitude and phase to follow the grid.

3. Control Challenges

  • Instability: Poor tuning can lead to oscillations between inverters
  • Over-reliance on GFM: Too few GFM inverters can cause voltage and frequency collapse
  • Uncoordinated response: Load changes may not be properly shared
