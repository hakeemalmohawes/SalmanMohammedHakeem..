1. Why Analytical Modeling?

High-power converters are essential in modern power systems, particularly in HVdc links, FACTS devices, and renewable energy integration. Analytical modeling allows engineers to design control systems, evaluate stability, and predict dynamic behavior with precision and efficiency.

2. Key Applications

  • HVdc (High Voltage Direct Current): Long-distance transmission of bulk power, requiring detailed converter modeling for control and protection.
  • FACTS (Flexible AC Transmission Systems): Voltage, power flow, and stability control tools that rely on converter dynamics.
  • Wind Energy Systems: Power electronic interfaces used for generator control and grid integration.

3. Role in System Studies

Analytical models provide simplified yet accurate representations of complex systems. They are used in small-signal stability analysis, control design, system identification, and validation. These models help engineers ensure that large converter-based systems behave reliably under various grid conditions.
