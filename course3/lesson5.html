1. Purpose of Stability Analysis

As inverters increasingly dominate power systems, ensuring small-signal and large-signal stability becomes critical. The duality framework helps identify and interpret the different contributions of GFM and GFL inverters to system behavior.

2. Applying Duality to Stability Models

By treating GFM and GFL inverters as dual systems ‚Äî one as a voltage source and the other as a current source ‚Äî control designers can map their interaction, coupling, and stability constraints. This simplifies multi-inverter system analysis.

3. Sensitivity and Placement

  • GFM Strength: The more GFM inverters and their capacity, the more robust the system
  • GFL Location: Improper placement can amplify disturbances if not supported by nearby GFM units
  • Control Coupling: Unstable eigenvalues may result from poorly tuned control loops or latency
