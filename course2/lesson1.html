1. Why Aim for 100% Renewables?

The global power sector is a major source of carbon emissions. Transitioning to a power system that runs entirely on renewables such as wind, solar, and hydro is seen as a cornerstone of climate change mitigation strategies. A 100% renewable grid would eliminate fossil fuel reliance, reduce emissions, and improve air quality.

2. The Technology Readiness

Technologies like utility-scale solar PV, onshore and offshore wind, grid-scale batteries, demand response, and advanced forecasting have matured significantly. These advances make it technically feasible to operate a reliable power system entirely from renewable sources.

3. Cost Competitiveness

Renewables have become the cheapest sources of new electricity in many markets. Solar and wind now undercut fossil fuels in terms of levelized cost of energy (LCOE), even before accounting for environmental benefits. Continued cost declines are expected as deployment scales.

4. Public and Policy Support

Governments, cities, and corporations worldwide have adopted net-zero or 100% renewable targets. These policies are accelerating investment and innovation in renewable infrastructure and operations.
