üåç 1. GFLs in Solar PV Systems

Grid-following inverters are the most common interface used in large-scale solar photovoltaic (PV) systems. They convert DC power from PV arrays into synchronized AC current, injecting it into the grid. Their cost-effectiveness and mature technology make them a reliable choice for utility-scale and residential applications.

Many wind turbines also use GFL inverters, especially in Type-4 configurations where the generator is fully decoupled from the grid via back-to-back converters. The GFL side ensures that current is injected according to grid voltage and frequency, while the generator side extracts variable-speed wind energy efficiently.

  • Strengths: Cost-effective, mature control algorithms, excellent for strong grids
  • Limitations: Poor performance during faults or in weak grids, cannot operate autonomously
