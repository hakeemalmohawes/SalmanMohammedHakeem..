1. What Are GFM and GFL Inverters?

Grid-Forming inverters establish the voltage and frequency reference for the grid. They are essential in islanded or low-inertia systems. Grid-Following inverters depend on an external voltage reference and adjust their output to follow the grid.

2. Control Philosophy and Duality

The term "duality" refers to the complementary nature of GFM and GFL inverters. While GFM devices act like voltage sources (setting the rhythm), GFL devices behave like current sources (dancing to the rhythm). They rely on different assumptions and measurements for their control loops.

3. Importance in Modern Power Systems

With growing renewable penetration, understanding the interaction between GFM and GFL inverters is critical. This course explores how their dual roles can be coordinated to maintain stability, especially in inverter-dominated grids.
