üî¢ 2. Virtual Oscillator Control (VOC)

Virtual Oscillator Control mimics the behavior of nonlinear oscillators to achieve synchronization. It offers improved performance in systems with rapid transients and low inertia, and can stabilize microgrids more effectively in some scenarios. VOC is particularly useful in systems with limited communication or where fast synchronization is critical.

‚åö 3. Synchronverters

Synchronverters emulate the full swing dynamics of synchronous generators, including inertia and damping. This approach gives GFMs a realistic machine-like response and facilitates integration into grids with conventional equipment. Synchronverters are more complex but offer better interaction with legacy protection systems.

üåê 4. Choice of Control Strategy

The choice between droop, VOC, or synchronverter depends on system objectives:

  • Use droop control for general-purpose decentralized load sharing.
  • Use VOC when fast synchronization and transient handling are priorities.
  • Use synchronverter when close integration with conventional generation and protection is needed.
