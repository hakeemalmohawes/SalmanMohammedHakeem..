1. Why Impedance Modeling?

Impedance models capture the frequency-dependent behavior of a converter and its interaction with the grid. These models are essential for studying stability, resonance, and controller interactions in weak or complex grids.

2. Converter Impedance Representation

Converters are represented using either input or output impedance, depending on the direction of analysis. Common formats include:

  • d-q impedance models: Represent converter dynamics in synchronous reference frame
  • Nyquist-based impedance: For frequency-domain stability assessment
  • Black-box impedance: Measured from simulations or hardware

3. Applications in System Studies

Impedance models help:

  • Evaluate grid strength and converter stability margins
  • Design robust controllers to avoid resonance
  • Understand multi-inverter interaction in weak grids
