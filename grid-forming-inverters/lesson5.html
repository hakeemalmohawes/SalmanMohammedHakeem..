1. Integration into Existing Systems

GFMs can be integrated into both legacy grids with synchronous machines and inverter-based microgrids. Their ability to regulate voltage and frequency locally makes them flexible in deployment. Coordination, however, is crucial to avoid control conflicts.

2. Coordinating with Other GFMs

When multiple GFMs are deployed, they must coordinate power sharing and dynamic response. Droop-based and oscillator-based methods enable decentralized sharing of active and reactive power. Communication may be used for improved precision but is not required for basic functionality.

4. Load and Fault Dynamics

GFMs must handle load fluctuations and fault events without loss of synchronism. This requires fast, stable control responses and sometimes supplemental services like inertia emulation and fault current limiting.
