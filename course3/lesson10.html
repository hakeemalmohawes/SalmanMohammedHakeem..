1. Summary of the Duality Framework

The duality theory highlights the symmetry between GFM and GFL inverters ‚Äî where one forms the grid and the other follows it. This concept allows structured analysis, design, and control of increasingly inverter-dominated grids.

2. Benefits in Power System Design

By organizing inverter control systems around the dual roles of forming and following, operators can design resilient, scalable, and efficient grids. The separation simplifies fault handling, stability analysis, and distributed energy resource integration.

3. Future Directions

  • Hybrid modes: Inverters that dynamically switch between GFM and GFL roles
  • Standardized control blocks: For rapid design and deployment of compliant systems
  • Distributed intelligence: Self-organizing inverter networks that adapt to grid needs
