  • GFLs are current-controlled inverters that rely on an external voltage reference
  • They synchronize using Phase-Locked Loops (PLLs)
  • Control is structured with outer power and inner current loops
  • They are common in grid-tied solar, wind, and battery systems

  • GFLs are not suitable for black start or voltage formation
