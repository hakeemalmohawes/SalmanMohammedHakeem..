1. HVdc Systems

HVdc systems use VSC or MMC topologies for long-distance power transmission. Modeling focuses on control loop dynamics, converter impedance, and grid interaction. Case studies often examine black-start capability, fault recovery, and interaction with weak AC systems.

2. FACTS Devices

FACTS controllers such as STATCOMs and SVCs are modeled to support voltage and reactive power control. Studies show their impact on damping oscillations and regulating voltage under varying grid loads. Analytical modeling helps design robust controls against fast disturbances.

3. Wind Power Systems

Modern wind turbines rely on full-scale converters interfacing with the grid. Models must capture generator-converter dynamics, PLL behavior, and ride-through performance. Small-signal and impedance analysis are crucial to ensure grid compliance and coordination with other inverters.
