üí° 1. Weak Grid Definition

A weak grid has low short-circuit power and high impedance, resulting in poor voltage and frequency stability. In such environments, the voltage waveform is more susceptible to distortion, sags, and harmonic interference.

‚ùå 2. GFL Performance Issues

  • PLL instability: GFLs rely on PLLs that may become erratic during disturbances
  • Current overshoot: Grid impedance can lead to overcurrent faults
  • Disconnection risk: GFLs may trip offline during faults or sag events

ü§û 3. Fault Response Limitations

In weak grids, GFLs may fail to ride through faults, and their limited control authority makes it hard to support grid recovery. Voltage and frequency excursions beyond allowed margins usually cause inverters to disconnect, potentially making grid restoration harder.

  • GFLs cannot set voltage or frequency references
  • They cannot form or maintain an islanded microgrid
  • They rely on external sources (e.g., synchronous machines or GFMs)

  • GFL inverters are limited in weak and unstable grids due to PLL dependency
  • They may disconnect during faults, leading to grid instability
  • They require strong reference sources to operate correctly
