1. The Importance of Transmission

Renewable energy sources like wind and solar are often located far from major demand centers. Strong transmission systems are required to move electricity across long distances, reduce curtailment, and enhance reliability.

2. Grid Bottlenecks and Constraints

Existing grids were not built for high VRE penetration. Congestion, aging infrastructure, and limited interregional connections can restrict the flow of renewable energy and lead to higher costs and inefficiencies.

3. Modernizing the Grid

  • High-voltage DC (HVDC) lines: Efficiently transmit electricity over long distances with minimal losses
  • Dynamic line rating: Uses real-time conditions to increase transmission capacity
  • Grid-enhancing technologies: Devices that help manage power flow and increase utilization of existing lines
  • Underground and offshore transmission: Enable new routes for clean energy delivery

4. Planning and Regulatory Challenges

Transmission projects can face long delays due to permitting, siting, and regulatory approval. Coordinated regional planning and faster permitting processes are key to accelerating grid expansion.
