1. The Vision for 100% Renewables

A 100% renewable grid is not just a technical goal but a transformative shift in how energy is produced, distributed, and used. It addresses climate change, energy security, and sustainable development. This vision requires aligning technology, policy, economics, and public support.

2. Key Enablers

  • Policy frameworks: Clear targets, regulations, and incentives guide investment
  • Technology innovation: Continuous R&D improves efficiency, reliability, and cost
  • Investment and finance: Mobilizing capital is essential to scale clean energy
  • Public support and education: Social acceptance and understanding drive change

3. Global Momentum

Many countries, states, and cities are already moving toward 100% renewable electricity. Examples include:

  • Iceland: Nearly 100% renewable electricity from hydro and geothermal
  • Denmark: High wind penetration and ambitious national targets
  • Hawaii: First U.S. state with a 100% renewable electricity law

4. The Path Forward

Achieving 100% renewables involves coordinated action across sectors, stakeholder engagement, and a flexible, resilient grid architecture. While challenges exist, the tools and momentum are in place to realize the transition.
