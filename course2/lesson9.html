1. Limitations of Current Market Models

Traditional electricity markets were designed for centralized, dispatchable fossil generation. These models may not properly value fast-acting, flexible resources like batteries, demand response, and inverter-based systems.

2. New Market Requirements

Markets in a renewable future need to:

  • Value flexibility and fast response
  • Enable participation by distributed and storage resources
  • Support long-term investment in clean technologies

3. Evolving Market Mechanisms

  • Capacity markets: Ensure resource adequacy by paying for availability, not just energy delivery
  • Ancillary services markets: Compensate for services like frequency regulation and voltage support
  • Real-time and locational pricing: Reflect local supply and demand conditions accurately

4. Enabling Distributed and Consumer Participation

Markets should allow prosumers, aggregators, and flexible loads to participate directly. This requires reducing entry barriers and enabling aggregation of small-scale resources to deliver system-level value.

5. Aligning Incentives with Grid Needs

Well-designed incentives and transparent price signals can encourage technologies that support system reliability, such as storage, demand response, and hybrid renewable systems.
