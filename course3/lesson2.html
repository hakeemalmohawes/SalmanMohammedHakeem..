1. Control-Oriented Modeling

GFM and GFL inverters require distinct modeling frameworks due to their different control roles. GFM inverters are typically modeled as voltage-controlled voltage sources with inner current loops and outer voltage and frequency control. In contrast, GFL inverters are modeled as current-controlled sources relying on phase-locked loops (PLLs) for synchronization.

2. Structural Differences

  • GFM: Has internal voltage reference, droop control, and synchronization by design.
  • GFL: Requires external grid reference; modeled with PLLs, current controllers, and feedforward loops.

3. Modeling Implications of Duality

Duality allows us to represent both inverter types under a unified framework where roles can be flipped under transformation. This is useful for stability studies, fault analysis, and inverter coordination strategies.
