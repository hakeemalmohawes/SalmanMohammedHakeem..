1. Importance of Stability Analysis

As converter-based generation grows, traditional assumptions about inertia and damping no longer apply. Analytical tools help ensure stability in scenarios with low short-circuit ratios and multiple interacting controllers.

2. Stability Assessment Techniques

  • Eigenvalue analysis: From small-signal models to evaluate oscillatory modes
  • Impedance ratio method: Using source/load impedance to assess robustness
  • Bode and Nyquist plots: Identify gain and phase margins for safe operation

3. Enhancing System Robustness

Stability is improved by:

  • Tuning control parameters to avoid resonances
  • Using virtual inertia or damping controls
  • Maintaining strong anchor points (e.g., GFM inverters or synchronous machines)
