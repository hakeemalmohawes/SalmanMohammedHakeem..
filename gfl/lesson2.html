For a Grid-Following Inverter to inject current into the grid, it must first align itself with the grid‚Äôs voltage waveform. This includes matching the phase, frequency, and magnitude. Synchronization ensures safe and efficient operation, preventing damage or instability.

ü™° 2. What Is a Phase-Locked Loop (PLL)?

The Phase-Locked Loop (PLL) is the core component used by GFL inverters to stay synchronized with the grid. It continuously monitors the grid voltage and adjusts the inverter‚Äôs output to lock onto the phase angle and frequency. If the grid frequency changes, the PLL tracks it and ensures the inverter stays in phase.

üöÄ 3. How the PLL Works

  • The inverter measures grid voltage (typically via abc-dq transformation)
  • The PLL detects the phase difference between grid voltage and the inverter reference
  • The error signal is passed through a PI controller to adjust the inverter phase
  • Eventually, the inverter‚Äôs phase and frequency match the grid

‚ö° 4. PLL Sensitivity and Weak Grids

In weak or distorted grids, the PLL may become unstable or inaccurate. Harmonics, voltage sags, or frequency swings can confuse the PLL, causing incorrect synchronization. This is a major reason GFL inverters struggle in systems with low inertia or poor power quality.

  • GFL inverters use PLLs to match the phase and frequency of the grid
  • Accurate synchronization is essential for current injection and stability
